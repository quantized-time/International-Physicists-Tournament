A DIY method of testing the charge of a battery is to drop it on a hard surface and observe whether it bounces. 
Explain the phenomenon and find other non-invasive mechanical methods to estimate the remaining charge. 
Optimize the accuracy of your method. https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=suH32X91sQE


"KineticVoltmeter_Report" is a concise report on our findings and explanations. Essentially, the DIY method works well (at least for alkaline batteries). We also propose a phase transition-like phenomenon in ZnO batteries to explain a sudden change in bounce height as charge varies. Lastly, we carry out another method by emitting and detecting a reflected ultrasonic wave as it travels through the battery. The times of flight are expected to be different for a full vs. empty battery, which is what we observe experimentally as well.
